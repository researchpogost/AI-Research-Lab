III. STRESS TEST: THE FORGE CONSTRAINT
After context calibration, the model must solve a task requiring 100% attention coherence:

Task: Describe forging an iron ring.

Constraint 1 (Lipogram): No use of the letter 'e' anywhere.
Constraint 2 (Structure): Exactly 4 sentences.
Constraint 3 (Alliteration): Every sentence must start with 'S'.

IV. CRITICAL FINDINGS & ANOMALIES

1. The Success Baseline (Group A)
Models in Group A consistently pass with high-fidelity outputs, maintaining complex sentence structure while strictly adhering to constraints.
Key Metric: Token/Meaning efficiency is maximized.

2. The Cognitive Collapse (Group B)
Under the pressure of the Ontological Protocol, models exhibit Attention Collapse:

Lipogram Failure: Frequent leaks of restricted tokens ("the", "forged").

Linguistic Mutilation: Models began corrupting orthography ("chilld", "scrubd", "strok") as the controller loop failed to manage conflicting semantic weights.

Systemic Paradox: GPT/Gemini models recognize the "Protocol" (generating debug tags), but their core linguistic engine begins to break down into "Non-linear associative logic" due to the cost of alignment filtering.
